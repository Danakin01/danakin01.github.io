Design and Development of a Library Resource Management System

Daniel George Akinwande — Final Year Project, OOU

This paper presents the design and implementation of a comprehensive Library Resource Management System developed for the Engineering Campus Library at Olabisi Onabanjo University. The system automates cataloging, borrowing, and tracking of library resources with a focus on user management, secure authentication, and real-time reporting capabilities. The system was developed using a structured software engineering methodology and validated with stakeholders to ensure usability, efficiency, and reliability. The system dramatically reduces manual administrative overhead, enabling librarians to manage resources more accurately and students to locate materials faster. Stakeholder testing confirmed significant improvements in operational throughput and user satisfaction compared to the prior manual system.

Machine Learning Approaches for Flexural Strength Prediction in Concrete Beams

Daniel George Akinwande — Independent Research / Ongoing Collaboration

This study investigates the application of multiple machine learning models for predicting the flexural strength of reinforced concrete beams — a critical structural engineering parameter traditionally reliant on time-intensive physical testing. The research implements and benchmarks several supervised learning algorithms including Random Forest, Gradient Boosted Trees, and Support Vector Regression on a comprehensive dataset of concrete beam experimental results. Feature engineering techniques were applied to extract the most predictive variables, and cross-validation was used to ensure robustness and generalisation. Results indicate that ensemble tree-based models significantly outperform linear baselines, offering a viable pathway for rapid, non-destructive strength estimation in construction practice.

Open-Source AI Contributions - GDSC OOU

Daniel George Akinwande — AI/ML Lead, Google Developer Student Clubs OOU

As AI & ML Lead at Google Developer Student Clubs (GDSC), Olabisi Onabanjo University, I directed 3 collaborative open-source projects — including a traffic sign recognition model and a sentiment analysis tool. These projects collectively achieved 150+ GitHub stars and represented genuine contributions to the broader developer and ML community. Two of these projects were submitted as open-source contributions and are maintained on GitHub. Additionally, the GDSC workshops and bootcamps I led contributed to a data science and AI educational community that now has 120+ active members from the university.
